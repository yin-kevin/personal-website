Notes

Included here are links to papers relevant to my research interests and resources I've used for coursework or self-study. Links may not all be stable. Credit for the resources goes to the respective authors. Papers are alphabetical by first author name while resources are in no particular order.

Interesting Papers

Acemoglu, Johnson, and Robinson (2001). "The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development: An Empirical Investigation". American Economic Review.
Acemoglu, Ozdaglar, and Tahbaz-Salehi (2013). "Systemic Risk and Stability in Financial Networks". NBER Working Papers.
Aguiar and Amador (2014). "Sovereign Debt". Handbook of International Economics.
Arellano (2008). "Default Risk and Income Fluctuations in Emerging Economies". American Economic Review.
Brandt and Rawski (2008). "China's Great Economic Transformation". Cambridge University Press.
Eaton and Gersovitz (1981). "Debt with Potential Repudiation: Theoretical and Empirical Analysis". Review of Economic Studies.
Eaton and Kortum (2002). "Technology, Geography, and Trade". Econometrica.
Elliot, Jackson, and Golub (2014). "Financial Networks and Contagion". American Economic Review.
Farhi and Werning (2017). "Fiscal Unions". American Economic Review.
Gorton and Metrick (2012). "Securitized Banking and the Run on Repo". Journal of Financial Economics.
Kiyotaki and Moore (1997). "Credit Cycles". Journal of Political Economy.
Mendoza (2010). "Sudden Stops, Financial Crises, and Leverage". American Economic Review.
Mendoza and Yue (2012). "A General Equilibrium Model of Sovereign Default and Business Cycles". Quarterly Journal of Economics.
Obstfeld and Rogoff (2000). "The Six Major Puzzles in International Macroeconomics: Is There a Common Cause?". NBER Working Papers.
Rajan (2006). "Has Financial Development Made the World Riskier?". NBER Working Papers.
Rajan and Zingales (1996). "Financial Dependence and Growth". NBER Working Papers.
Reinhart and Rogoff (2009). "The Aftermath of Financial Crises". NBER Working Papers.
Sims (1980). "Macroeconomics and Reality". Econometrica.

Resources

Quant Econ. - A set of tools and documentation for computational economics and econometrics.
John Cochrane's Blog. - For thoughts on the macroeconomy, global finance, and politics.
Chris Blattman's Blog. - For thoughts on political economy, war, and academia.
Noah Smith's Blog. - For meta-commentary on economics.
Jakub Arnold's Blog. - For refreshers on machine learning theory.
Joseph Chang's "Stochastic Processes". - An unfinished textbook on stochastic processes.
Matrix Cookbook. - A comprehensive set of rules for matrix calculus.
Matrix Calculator. - When the cookbook fails.
Notes on Dirichlet Distribution. - The Dirichlet distribution is the conjugate prior for the multinomial and categorical distributions.
Notes on Time Series. - Graduate level introduction to time series.
