A Few Places I've Been

Map

Photos

Canadian Rockies, 2017

I found a job as a busboy in the lobby of the Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge the first summer after college. In spite of being only five hours from where I grew up, the Rockies still feel foreign if you're from the Prairies. I met great people at work, caught the last day of the ski season in May, and hiked every peak in the valley. 'Old Man' is by far the best, even though we got caught in the cold and rain the second time. Mount Athabasca, at 3491 meters tall and just over 100km from town, is also where I cut my teeth at mountaineering. Poor weather conditions nearly prevented a summit but we managed.

Western Europe, 2017

Since it was my first solo trip I forgot to bring a real camera. A month was spent wading in and out of homes and hostels in a trip spanning the UK, France, Belgium, Germany, Poland, the Czech Republic, Austria, and the Netherlands. I caught up with friends in Belgium and the Netherlands since a few were studying in the Hague or congregating in Brussels. Auschwitz was a poignant stop. Climbing Mount Athabasca that same summer gave me confidence to attempt the Grossglockner, the tallest mountain in Austria at 3,798 meters. Summiting it remains the highlight of the trip. A friend from Toronto caught me in Paris on the way back where we had a memorable drink at the Bar Hemingway.

China, 2018

From the beginning of May to the end of August 2018, I worked marketing at a French wine company in Shanghai while living with my dad. Having only ever spent sub-month-long intervals in China and even less in Shanghai specifically, it was liberating to have the time to properly explore and try to resuscitate my Mandarin. I took a month off in July to visit family in Taiyuan, Shanxi, and in Huangguang, Hubei, as well as a sojourn to Nanjing where my parents went to college. I also made my first trip to Xian, accompanied by my grandparents. Nanjing duck blood soup, the Terracota Army, and Shanghai's numerous speakeasies were highlights.

Southern Africa, 2019

In the summer before senior year, I lived in Johannesburg with my friend and his family, doing research for his mum's think tank. I travelled around the region, covering Botswana, Eswatini, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Namibia. In Botswana I caught up with an old friend, who organized a week-long camping safari in Kalahari Desert that I won't ever forget. In Mozambique we took a boat to a remote peninsula off the coast of Maputo where we stayed at a “bed and breakfast” of sorts, run by a retired civil war revolutionary. In Namibia I visited to Sossusvlei and rented a 4x4 to drive up the Skeleton Coast for a solo camping trip, soaking out four invigorating months. Spare tires were essential.

New York, San Francisco, 2021

With pandemic travel restrictions keeping me state-side in 2021 and the prospect of remote work delaying the need to return, I left New Haven to couch surf for two-plus months with friends in New York and San Francisco. I enjoyed NY Dosas' special pondicherry as seen on Munchies and the negroni I had at Dante. We spent a day hiking up Vernal Falls in Yosemite and a memorable night sleeping in the car. We spent another weekend tasting wines in Napa, the best of which was Kenzo Estate. Photos from the NY half are sparse since it wasn't my first time.
