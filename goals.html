About

Kevin Yin is a Trudeau Scholar, SSHRC Doctoral Fellow, and economics Ph.D. student at the University of California, Berkeley. His doctoral research lies at the intersection of macroeconomic growth, international economics and macro-finance. Kevin's work seeks to understand how sovereign debt defaults occur in emerging markets, how bank network structures emerge and the corresponding consequences for financial stability, and the general equilibrium costs of supply chain shocks in wartime.

Kevin is also a Senior Fellow at the Asia Pacific Foundation, a Research Fellow at the Macro Policy Lab, and was named one of Forbes' 30 Under 30 in 2026. He has contributed his views on the economy and global affairs for the Financial Times, Foreign Affairs, and The Globe and Mail. Previously Kevin worked at Bridgewater Associates and the Bank of England. He holds an M.A. in economics from Yale University and a B.A. from the University of Toronto where he studied economics, mathematics, and philosophy.
