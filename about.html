About

Kevin Yin is an economics PhD student at the University of California, Berkeley and SHRCC Doctoral Fellow. His interests lie at the intersection of macroeconomic growth, international economics and macro-finance. In particular, Kevin's research seeks to understand how sovereign debt defaults occur in emerging markets, how bank network structures emerge and the corresponding consequences for financial stability, and how we can think about formally analyzing the tradeoffs of real world policy ideas like European Union debt mutualization.

In his spare time, Kevin also contributes opinion pieces on the economy and global affairs for The Globe and Mail. He was previously a Tobin Pre-Doctoral Fellow under Costas Arkolakis and Conor Walsh at Yale University. He holds an M.A. in economics from Yale and a B.A. in economics, philosophy, and mathematics from the University of Toronto.
